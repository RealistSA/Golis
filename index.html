Our Story

Where Somalia
meets London

Golis Restaurant is named after the majestic Golis mountain range of northern Somalia — a symbol of strength, heritage, and beauty. We bring that same spirit to every dish we serve.

Our kitchen is built on generations of Somali cooking tradition: fragrant xawaash spice blends, slow-cooked meats, hand-rolled canjeero, and the warmth of communal dining.

Whether you're Somali and craving a taste of home, or curious and trying it for the first time — you're welcome at our table.

The Menu

Traditional Somali dishes prepared with authentic spices and techniques, served with pride.

Signature · Mains

Hilib Ari

Tender slow-braised goat meat seasoned with xawaash — Somalia's iconic spice blend of cumin, coriander, turmeric and cardamom. Served with basmati rice.

Classic · Mains

Hilib Suqaar

Diced beef or chicken sautéed with onions, peppers, cumin and coriander. A staple of Somali street food culture — fast, bold, deeply satisfying.

From £10 Halal

Traditional · Mains

Maraq

Rich Somali bone broth simmered for hours with goat bones, garlic, cumin and lime. Served as a starter or a comforting main — deeply restorative.

From £8 Halal

Festive · Rice

Bariis Iskukaris

The crown jewel of Somali cuisine. Fragrant basmati rice cooked in seasoned broth with xawaash spices, topped with tender slow-cooked goat and caramelised onions.

From £14 Signature

Classic · Pasta

Baasto

Somali-style pasta — a beloved legacy of Italian colonial contact — simmered in a spiced tomato and meat sauce with East African aromatics. Comforting and unique.

Side · Rice

Bariis Pilau

Lightly spiced pilau rice cooked with whole cardamom pods, cloves and cinnamon. The perfect companion to any Golis main dish.

Breakfast · Classic

Canjeero

Somalia's beloved fermented flatbread — spongy, slightly sour, with a distinctive bubbly texture. Served with honey, sesame butter (sugo), or meat stew.

From £6 Traditional

Breakfast · Bread

Muufo

Thick, hearty Somali flatbread made from maize flour — crispy on the outside, soft within. Traditionally eaten in the morning with tea and suqaar.

From £4 Traditional
